ETF Descriptions

Pimco Active Bond Exchange-Traded Fund (BOND) is a well-diversified fund including government related, securitized and investment grade corporate debt. 75% of the portfolio holdings being investment-grade credit ratings, and 65% boast AAA ratings.

----AAA rated---Lowest Risk----

Vanguard FTSE Europe ETF (VGK) has various stocks from many different sectors, with its three biggest industries being financial services, healthcare and industrials. It is advantageous because of its numerous stocks and industries across the European markets, aiming to spread investor risk appropriately.

 ------Moderate Volatality-----

SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust (SPY) trades on the NYSE Arca and is designed to track the S&P 500 stock market index. The S&P 500 represents 80% of the North American market and includes companies across all sectors. It is frequently used as a benchmark for the market as whole.

------Moderate Volatality-----

Schwab Emerging Markets Equity ETF (SCHE) invests in emerging stocks as well as emerging countries such as China, Taiwan, India and Brazil. Its top sectors are financial services, technology and consumer cyclical.

------Higher Volatality-----

Vanguard Russell 1000 Growth ETF (VONG) holds a portfolio of approximately 500 prominent U.S. companies that exhibit substantial growth prospects. A substantial 46.30% of the fund's assets are allocated to the technology sector. This ETF is closely tied to the Russell 1000 Growth Index, providing access to large-cap enterprises operating within the growth-oriented segment of the U.S. stock market. Companies within this growth category present significant profit potential as they are in their initial developmental stages, but this also elevates the associated risk level for this asset class.

------Higher Volatality-----
